My Current Top 3 Ski Resorts In Switerland!

This part of the website will break down my favorite ski resorts in Switzerland, providing information on price, location, skiing difficulty, ski area size, and a language breakdown.

#1 Arosa

Arosa in Lezerheide, Switzerland
Rating
Ski Area: 225km
Lifts: 42
Top Height: 2865m
Area Difficulty: 4/5
Price: $$
Airport Distance: 2 Hours

Arosa is my favorite ski area in Switzerland. Arosa is a ski area that is not very popular with international tourists. It is more for nearby local swiss residents, meaning it is crowded as other resorts. They also have great food options in restaurants with some of the best views I have ever seen. Nevertheless, this resort is not friendly for beginners but will provide an amazing experience for experienced skiers looking for long slopes, which will allow fast-past carving. The price for multiple days when booking advance is also very modest, and that is not because of lower lift quality as they have some of the newest lifts in Switzerland. Including the fattest gondola in Europe.

#2 Champéry

Champéry in Vallais, Switzerland
Rating
Ski Area: 600km
Lifts: 209
Top Height: 2,466m
Area Difficulty: 3/5
Price: $$$
Airport Distance: 1.5 Hours

Champéry is where I initially spent most of my younger years learning to ski, and I can say that it has something for everyone. I have been skiing there for ten years have still not skied every slope Champéry is part of the Portes Du Soleil ski area, one of the world's biggest ski areas, as it links 12 different pistes together. Moreover, this resort also shares its Ski Area with France, allowing Skiers to go to France and experience some exceptional cheap food. Champéry provides a great place to learn skiing as the area has over 39 green runs and offers excellent challenges for advanced skiers, such as the Mur Suisse. The Mur Suisse is one of the steepest slopes in Europe. This resort gets popular in the holiday weeks and is not as high as other Swiss ski resorts.

#3 Zermatt

Zermatt in Vallais, Switzerland
Rating
Ski Area: 360km
Lifts: 54
Top Height: 3,899m
Area Difficulty: 4/5
Price: $$$$
Airport Distance: 2.5 Hours

Despite the classic cliché, Zermatt is one of the best options in Switzerland. If you are traveling to Switzerland and are looking to get a unique experience, Zermatt does provide a dreamy experience. Being the highest ski area in Switzerland, you will get the white Christmas you are after, with a luxurious touch. Zermatt has luxury VIP gondolas, Michelin star restaurants, and beautiful boutique hotels while also providing exceptional skiing, as they height ensure attractive conditions all year round. Zermatt has no cars as they are banned, providing a refreshing atmosphere. Zermatt currently has the longest run in Europe, which is 20KM long. Zermatt also has a lot of opportunities for people just looking to enjoy the Swiss Alps, including ice skating, tobogganing, curling, and snowing. Did I forget to mention that the resort is also connected to Italy...
